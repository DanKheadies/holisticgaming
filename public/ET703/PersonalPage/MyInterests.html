My Interests

My biggest interests involve games and play, and I'm working to align my professional goals with my personal interests.

I graduated from the University of South Carolina's Honors College in 2013 with a major in Biology, a minor in Psychology, and a focus in Educational Gaming. After pursing Medical School and Biomedical Engineering, I decided to shift my time and energy towards games and education. During my undergraduate career, I worked on a number of research projects:

  • Learning About Learning: Cognitive Gaming as a Technology of the Self
    • Which investigates the inherent learning architectures in video games and video game play as well as the various cognitive and meta-cognitive gains
  • Immunis: An Immunology Game
    • A novel approach to educate players using real-world content
  • A Review of the Cognitive and Physiological Effects of Video Games and Potential Uses in Research, Treatment, and Education
    • Which reviews various academic resources for video game and video game play affects on physical and psychological processes
  • Electronic Learning Modules
    • Which designed innovative approaches for conveying and producing educational content with new, available technologies

Through this collaborative work with other students, friends, and teachers as well as a lot of independent reading, I wrote my senior thesis on Holistic Gaming (1.4 MB): a game design philosophy that works to understand the physical and psychological relationship between humans, video games, and video game play in order to design games that facilitate education, research, healthcare, and human development.

I've been fortunate to grow up playing a variety of games. Back in Spartanburg, I played competitive (club) soccer for ~11 years with 4 years of high school soccer. I've played intermurals at USC for the last 6 years, and I joined the International Futbol Club team at USC for 1 and 1/2 years. I play racquetball, disc golf, and golf when I can find the time. I've played numerous card games, such as Cards Against Humanity, poker, and the like, dice games, e.g. Craps, Liars Dice, etc., and board games, like Risk, Stratego, Chess, Chinese checkers, and so on. A couple of friends and I invented a drinking board game--Explosions in My Pants: A Gentleman's Game. And of course video games... So. Many. Video games. Collectively, these experiences have helped guide me into a field of study that is challenging, worthwhile, and fun.

Currently, I am working as a User Experience Designer at The Mariner Group. We are responsible for designing and developing security technologies for a number of domains, e.g. maritime ports and harbors, border security, and airports. We have co-workers across the country, and clients across the world. We even helped cover security at Nelson Mandela's funeral in South Africa. We have recently merged with Ares Security Corporation, which offers innovative and effective solutions to plan, detect, assess, prevent, and respond to all types of incidents. I enjoy working with Mariner and Ares because I'm learning how to design pragmatic technologies for a wide audience of Users. I hope to continue working with them until I am ready to develop and deploy my holistic video games. One of my favorite aspects of my job is being able my bike to work.
